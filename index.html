Tim Berners-Lee

The father of World Wide Web

Sir Tim Berners Lee's time line

  • June 8, 1955 Tim Berners-Lee was born in London, England to parents who were early computer scientists, working on one of the earliest computers

  • 1973-1976 He attended Queen's College, Oxford, where he received a first-class degree in physics

  • 1980 Berners-Lee started working at CERN as a freelance consultant for the physics research facility

  • 1980-1984 He worked for Image Computer Systems Ltd., located in Ferndown, Dorset, where he designed a variety of computer systems

  • 1984 He returned to CERN to work on the design of the laboratory’s computer network, developing procedures that allowed diverse computers to communicate with one another and researchers to share their results and techniques

  • March 1989 He submitted his first proposal for what became the World Wide Web

  • 1990-1991 He wrote the first web client and server, marking the creation of the World Wide Web

  • 1991 He released his WWW software, which included the ‘line-mode’ browser, Web server software, and a library for developers

  • October 1994 Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C)

  • 1999 He became the first holder of the 3Com Founders chair at the Laboratory for Computer Science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

  • 2004 He was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II

  • 2007 He was awarded the Order of Merit

  • 2009 He was elected a foreign associate of the National Academy of Sciences

  • 2017 He was awarded the ACM A.M. Turing Prize

  • September 2022 He won the Seoul Peace Prize

"Tim Berners-Lee invented the Web. Without him, you wouldn't be reading this online article right now."

-- Marc Andreessen, American entrepreneur, investor, and software engineer, best known as the co-author of Mosaic, the first widely used Web browser
